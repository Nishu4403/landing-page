Vintage Gibson SG

Description
NECK

One-piece mahogany neck with nicely aged cream binding & tortoise side-dots. The profile of the neck gets chunkier as you work your way up the fretboard. Beautiful rosewood fingerboard with mother of pearl trapezoid inlays. Built with Kluson Deluxe tuners. 1 9/16" nut width; 24 3/4" scale length.

BODY

Solid, one-piece mahogany body with Maestro tremolo tailpiece & ABR-1 bridge. 4-ply, large black pickguard is in great shape with glossy look.Guitar weighs 7 lbs 15 oz.

PICKUPS

Gibson Pat No humbuckers. Bridge - 7.32K ohms; Neck - 7.44K ohms. Dual volume & tone controls with black "top hat" knobs; 3-way toggle switch.

In many ways, 1968 marks the end of what vintage guitar lovers consider the Golden Era of Gibson electrics. There's generally some truth to that, as ownership changed hands not too long after '68, bringing on many changes that would impact the company's storied and frankly legendary history. There are always exceptions of course, but in looking at the arc of Gibson's success, 1968 marked a pivotal year while delivering some excellent guitars.
